## 1. Platform redesign: move to AWS Bedrock for client confidentiality

**Why now.** The current production build of the California Law Chatbot routes generation through OpenRouter (Google Gemini) and uses direct OpenAI calls for embeddings. Neither provider today offers contractual Zero Data Retention terms sufficient to satisfy California Rule of Professional Conduct 1.6 and ABA Formal Opinion 512 when the attorney workflow includes client-identifying facts. In short: the tool is safe for general legal research, but not for client matters. Closing that gap is the gating item for F&F to actually use the system on client work.

**What we are doing.** Redesigning the application to run Anthropic Claude on AWS Bedrock inside an AWS account F&F controls (directly, or hosted by Dancing Elephant with F&F as tenant). Compliance characteristics of this path:

- Bedrock inference runs inside AWS's SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 security boundary.
- Anthropic personnel have **zero operator access** to Bedrock inference infrastructure.
- Bedrock invocation logging is disabled by default; we can opt-in to CloudWatch logging inside F&F's own account for supervision.
- Models in use on this build: Claude Opus 4.6 (Speed mode), Claude Sonnet 4.6 (Accuracy generator), Claude Haiku 4.5 (verifier and research planner).

**Scope of the redesign.** Beyond the model switch, the redesign enforces a confidentiality boundary the current build does not have:

- Server-enforced separation between "Accuracy" (client-safe) and "Speed" (non-client passthrough) workflows — the backend, not the UI, decides what can run where.
- Legislative and current-law sources are retained as first-class citations (previously dropped).
- A sanitization layer (in progress) will tokenize client-identifying facts before any external retrieval call, then re-hydrate only inside the trusted application boundary.
- Audit logging scrubs query bodies; only KMS-keyed hashes and source-usage metadata are retained.

**Where we are.** Foundations and recall restoration (Phases 1–5 of the implementation plan) are complete in a preview build running on AWS Bedrock. A preview URL is available for F&F to exercise. Sanitization (Phase 6) and the user-facing confidentiality banner / attestation (Phase 8) are the next two work items. Production cutover will require F&F's explicit go/no-go after live UAT with synthetic data, then a small attorney pilot.

**What we need from F&F.** Direction on AWS account ownership (F&F-owned vs. Dancing-Elephant-hosted under F&F contract), designation of the pilot attorney group, and a target UAT window.

## 2. Engagement agreement between Dancing Elephant and femme & femme

We should put a written agreement in place covering this work before production handoff. The work to date has been on good-faith understanding; the compliance posture of the finished product — and F&F's ability to represent it to clients and insurers — benefits from a formal engagement. Key items to cover:

- **Scope of work** — phased deliverables and acceptance criteria for each phase.
- **Fees and billing** — project-based or hourly, invoicing cadence, any success-based milestones.
- **Intellectual property** — ownership of the codebase, templates, sanitizer, audit schemas; reusable-module license-back to Dancing Elephant.
- **Data handling** — AWS account ownership, who pays infrastructure bills, retention policy for any artifacts generated by the tool, F&F's right to audit.
- **Confidentiality** — mutual NDA covering both F&F client information and Dancing Elephant methods/source code.
- **Warranties and limitations** — the tool provides legal information and drafting assistance, not legal advice; supervised attorney review is required for any client work product.
- **Insurance** — Dancing Elephant professional liability / E&O coverage; F&F confirmation that its coverage includes supervision of AI-assisted work product.
- **Term and termination** — notice periods, wind-down support, orderly handoff of the AWS account and any hosted data.
- **Governing law and venue** — California, San Francisco County.
